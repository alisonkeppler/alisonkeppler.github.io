REDLINE

The problem: Redline’s site is leaning toward outdated, with minimal content and some challenging navigation. While the gym actively strives for a bare-bones atmosphere as a differentiator from other facilities, focusing on the sport over any other superfluous elements, there’s room to bring the site’s structure and information up a notch.

 The goal*: Work through a creative concept update process, moving through the UX research and strategy to inform an enhanced vision for how the site can function more effectively in front of the target audience, while maintaining the gym’s signature personality.

*While recognizing there are many factors in play for the company that impact the site of which I'm unaware.

RESEARCH & ANALYSIS.

The first step was getting a stronger feel for Redline’s core customer base, and what it is they’re looking for from the gym/its site. I did some observations at the gym and spoke with a number of the students, using the pictured script to guide the discussions and maintain consistently comparable feedback. After reviewing and analyzing commonalities in those I spoke with, I created personas (example shown here) for two of the primary user types to further guide the strategy and development of the site’s trajectory.

I also gathered members of the target demographic to conduct a features brainstorm and open card sort, subsequently identifying patterns and expectations for optimal site content and architecture, including the need for more information about classes and instructors, and FAQs about the gym and boxing.

After getting a sense of the users and what they’re hoping to encounter throughout the site, I dove deeper into a user flow for that initial sign-up, as well as a customer journey map for preliminary acquisition (both pictured), to further clarify the specific steps and architecture that need to be in place to facilitate audience growth.

WIREFRAMES.

The wireframes for the homepage and class schedule shown here are structured in such a way as to cleanly highlight the extended content identified in the initial research and analysis, without potentially changing the overall stylization in a way that could disrupt the gym’s intended atmosphere and aesthetic.

VALIDATE & ITERATE.

As this was a creative concept only and not work in any official capacity for the gym, I didn't have the opportunity to implement any designs for legitimate testing, beyond informal positive feedback and discussion with the target audience. If I did have the chance to follow through with these recommendations and structures, I would montitor key performance indicators for the updates, including page visits for the new content and flows through the various resources, and would conduct moderated and open usability reviews for the new materials to ensure everything was running smoothly with the users.

Keep testing. Find a way to the solution.
