CCA

The problem: The partnership corrections organization features a wide range of valuable content on its site, but a fair amount is repetitive or structurally difficult to navigate, meaning there's risk of critical information falling through the cracks for readers.

â€¨The goal: In a freelance role for design and development agency Cloud Construct, work with the team's UX lead to analyze and organize CCA's information architecture and site structure, optimizing the content placement and navigation to ensure a streamlined experience for site visitors.

RESEARCH.

As this was a short project on a strict budget, the research portion focused primarily on carefully analyzing the existing site structure in comparison with best practices and competitive offerings, as well as taking a deeper dive into the specifics of the content across the site to identify opportunities for condensed or integrated pages and more user-friendly navigation flows.

WIREFRAMES.

After working with the agency's UX lead to confirm a strategy and recommendations for IA and site navigation, as well as receiving initial sign-off from the client on the proposed direction, I built out wireframes to more clearly depict the specific changes we were suggesting. The screens show the structural changes across the main nav bar, side menus and footers on desktop and mobile screens, including reasoning behind individual updates, with an additional page to document specific content moves across indiviudal pages. We went through one round of feedback, incorporating adjustments before settling on the documentation shown here.

DESIGN, VALIDATE & ITERATE.

This project was specifically designated for content strategy and wireframes only, with no additional needs for further aesthetic work or testing. The final deliverables were put into motion by the client, but had there been the opportunity for further analysis, I would measure the impact of the changes by monitoring and adjusting based on pre-established metrics, including page views, user movement throughout the site and heat mapping across priority pages.

Keep testing. Find a way to the solution.
