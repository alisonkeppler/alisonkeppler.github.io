 The problem: IMDb has an established market presence, but is overextending its content and glossing over its core competencies, potentially leading to missed opportunities for further user engagement.

The hypothesis: Most users are going to IMDb to find specific information about actors and movies – everything else is superfluous.

The goal: Streamline the mobile app to more clearly align with user priorities and reflect the key audience motivators.

RESEARCH.

I conducted several user interviews to find out whether or not people are aware of and using the IMDb app, and if so, what’s motivating them. I quickly confirmed my hypothesis; all users responded with some variation of the fact that yes, they use IMDb, primarily when prompted by a specific need or question about a particular actor or movie. Additional IMDb offerings, such as photo galleries and movie showtimes, are going untouched.

WIREFRAMES.

Based on the user feedback, I focused the wireframes on cutting down on some of the excess noise sprinkled throughout the app, and streamlining navigation to allow users to more easily uncover the information they need. After a few rounds of sketches and ongoing user check-ins, I settled on the above version that takes into account the secondary user preferences I uncovered for filmography and trivia.

DESIGN.

The pictured mockups for a revamped app homepage and several of the other priority screens reflect the cleaner, modern feel that will help IMDb hone in more directly on why users have come to the app, and what will keep them engaged throughout their experiences.

VALIDATE.

While this was a creative concept only, and I was as such unable to do any live testing of the new designs, I did get user validation confirming these styles and architectures would be well received. If possible, my next step would be to test the performance of the app based on specific user tasks and engagements, such as conducting usability testing and measuring clicks throughout user flows.

ITERATE.

Adjust the app content and functionality based on user feedback during testing.

Rinse. Repeat. Find a way to the solution.
